The context

Our marae is already connected. Now what?

Te Puni Kōkiri has funded broadband connectivity to over 75% of eligible marae across Aotearoa — including ours. The infrastructure is in. The WiFi is on.

But there is no funded programme teaching our whānau what to do with that connection. Training is explicitly out of scope for the current programme.

"The gap is not infrastructure. The gap is capability. What happens when a connected marae wants to build something?"

This proposal is about filling that gap — starting here, at Parikino.

The day

What rangatahi will do

1
Find a real problem

Rangatahi identify something that frustrates them at the marae, in their community, or in daily life.

2
Design before building

They plan their app — what it does, who uses it, what it shows — before touching any code.

3
Build it with AI

Using Claude AI on their phone, they generate a real working app. No coding knowledge needed.

4
Present and win

Teams present their live app. Judged on: real problem, working app, marae ready.
