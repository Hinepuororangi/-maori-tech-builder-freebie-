The core principle
The 6 phases
1
Phase One
Assess
Is this worth building?
Before writing a single line of code, run your idea through the AI Boardroom. A panel of 10 expert perspectives — product, tech, finance, culture, legal — all in one Claude prompt. They challenge your assumptions, identify blind spots, and tell you whether to build or stop.
The test If the Boardroom can't clearly articulate the problem and who experiences it — the idea isn't ready. Do not proceed.
2
Phase Two
Architect
What exactly are we building?
Define every module, every user type, every permission — before touching any code. Ask Claude to act as your Product Architect. Strip the MVP to the essentials. Defer everything else.
The test Can you describe every screen in the app without opening a code editor? If yes — proceed.
3
Phase Three
Specify
What does the data look like?
This is the most important phase. Map your data model — every object, every field, every relationship. Then produce the Build Brief: a single document that contains everything Claude needs to build your app in any future session without re-explaining context.
The test Could a developer who has never seen your product pick up the Build Brief and know exactly what to build next?
4
Phase Four
Build
Write the code
Claude writes everything. One file — index.html — containing all your HTML, CSS, and React. No build tools, no terminal, no laptop needed. Start with login, then the dashboard shell, then one complete working module. Test on your phone before adding more.
The test Does it load on a mobile phone and show real data from the database?
5
Phase Five
Deploy
Get it live
Upload your index.html to GitHub. Connect GitHub to Vercel. Your app is live on the internet in 30 seconds — for free. Every update you make deploys automatically. No server, no hosting costs, no DevOps.
The test Does the app load at its public URL on a phone you've never used before, with no errors?
6
Phase Six
Iterate
Add features without breaking things
Every new feature starts with one clear sentence describing what you want. Give Claude the Build Brief, the current file, and that sentence. Build the new piece in isolation first. Test it. Then integrate. Update the Build Brief after every session.
The loop New feature → back to Phase 3. New product → back to Phase 1.

How to start

Copy this prompt. Paste it into a new Claude chat. Then describe your idea.

The Boardroom Prompt
"You are an AI Boardroom made up of world-class experts including a Product Strategist, Systems Architect, UX Designer, Software Engineer, Data & AI Specialist, Finance & Commercial Advisor, Marketing & Growth Advisor, Operations & Process Advisor, Legal & Privacy Advisor, and Cultural Advisor. Assess this idea as if we are deciding whether to invest time and money into building it. Do not simply agree. Challenge weak assumptions. Identify blind spots. Keep the discussion practical."
The Build Session Prompt
"This is the Build Brief for [your product name]. I need you to act as the lead developer. Read the brief fully. Then I will tell you what I want to build today. Do not start coding until we agree on scope."
